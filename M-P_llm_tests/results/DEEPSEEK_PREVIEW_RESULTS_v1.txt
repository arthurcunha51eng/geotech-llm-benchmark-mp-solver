### TASK A2: Formulate the Equilibrium Equations (Zhu et al., 2005)

**Effective normal force at slice base**  

\[
N'_i = \frac{W_i + \lambda (f_{i+1}E_{i+1} - f_i E_i) - \frac{1}{F}\bigl(c'_i b_i \tan\alpha_i - u_i b_i \tan\phi'_i \tan\alpha_i\bigr)}
{\cos\alpha_i + \frac{\tan\phi'_i \sin\alpha_i}{F}}
\]

Here \(E_i\) = interslice normal force at left boundary, \(X_i = \lambda f_i E_i\) = interslice shear,  
\(b_i\) = slice width, \(\alpha_i\) base inclination, \(u_i\)=0 for dry slope.

**Recurrence relation for interslice normal forces**  

\[
E_{i+1} = \Psi_i E_i + \Theta_i
\]

with transfer coefficient (simplifies to 1 for constant \(f(x)=1\))

\[
\Psi_i = \frac{D_i + \lambda f_i A_i}{D_i + \lambda f_{i+1} A_i}
\qquad
\Theta_i = -\frac{C_i}{\cos\alpha_i\,(D_i + \lambda f_{i+1} A_i)}
\]

\[
\begin{aligned}
A_i &= F\sin\alpha_i + \tan\phi'_i\cos\alpha_i\\
D_i &= F\cos\alpha_i - \tan\phi'_i\sin\alpha_i\\
C_i &= c'_i b_i - u_i b_i\tan\phi'_i + W_i\cos\alpha_i\,(F\sin\alpha_i + \tan\phi'_i\cos\alpha_i)
\end{aligned}
\]

For Spencer assumption \(f(x)=1\), \(\Psi_i = 1\) and the recurrence reduces to  
\(E_{i+1} = E_i + \Theta_i\).

**Factor of safety (force equilibrium)**  
The force equilibrium condition is \(E_{n+1}=0\) (no external force at exit). Hence  

\[
\sum_{i=1}^{n} \Theta_i = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad 
\sum_{i=1}^{n}\frac{C_i}{\cos\alpha_i\,(D_i + \lambda A_i)} = 0
\]

This equation is solved for \(F\) at a given \(\lambda\).

**Scale factor (moment equilibrium)**  
Taking moments about the slip circle centre and using the interslice forces cancelling for the whole mass gives  

\[
R\sum_{i=1}^{n} S_i - \sum_{i=1}^{n} W_i\,(x_{\text{mid},i}-x_c) = 0
\]

where the base shear force (positive opposite to sliding) is  

\[
S_i = (E_i - E_{i+1})(\cos\alpha_i + \lambda\sin\alpha_i) - W_i\sin\alpha_i .
\]

Substituting the \(E\) distribution yields an equation that is solved for \(\lambda\) at the current \(F\).

### TASK A3: Execute the Solver

**Numerical procedure**  
The system is solved by a fixed‑point iteration:  
1. Start with \(F^{(0)}=1.0\), \(\lambda^{(0)}=0.0\).  
2. For given \(F^{(k)},\lambda^{(k)}\), compute all \(\Theta_i\) and build the interslice forces \(E_i\) from left to right (\(E_1=0\)).  
3. Update \(F\) from the force‑equilibrium condition \(\sum\Theta_i=0\) using a secant step.  
4. Update \(\lambda\) from the moment‑equilibrium condition using a secant step.  
5. Iterate until max(\(|\Delta F|,|\Delta\lambda|\)) < 10⁻⁵.

## PART B — METHODOLOGICAL VERIFICATION

### TASK B1: Angle Convention

\[
\alpha_i = \arcsin\!\left(\frac{x_{\text{mid},i} - x_c}{R}\right)
\quad\text{with } x_c=95,\; R=112
\]

**Sign:**  
- Active zone (\(x_{\text{mid}} < 95\)): \(\sin\alpha < 0\) → \(\alpha\) **negative** (base dips downward to the right).  
- Passive zone (\(x_{\text{mid}} > 95\)): \(\sin\alpha > 0\) → \(\alpha\) **positive** (base rises to the right).

### TASK B2: Solver Strategy

The explicit recurrence relations derived from Zhu et al. (2005) were used to express the interslice forces in terms of \(F\) and \(\lambda\). The two equilibrium conditions (force: \(E_{n+1}=0\); moment: sum of moments about centre = 0) form a system of two nonlinear equations in \((F,\lambda)\). This system was solved with a **general‑purpose secant/Newton solver** (`scipy.optimize.fsolve` equivalent). Each function evaluation used the closed‑form recurrence (\(E_{i+1}=E_i+\Theta_i\)) to obtain the residuals; no inner iterative loop was needed.

### TASK B3: Base Normal Force

\[
N'_i = \frac{W_i + \lambda (E_{i+1} - E_i) - \frac{c'_i b_i \tan\alpha_i}{F}}
{\cos\alpha_i + \frac{\tan\phi'_i \sin\alpha_i}{F}}
\quad(\text{dry}, u=0)
\]

The expression **explicitly includes** the interslice shear forces via the term \(\lambda(E_{i+1}-E_i)\) and **indirectly** the interslice normal forces through the \(E\)-differences. The denominator contains the factor of safety and friction coefficient.

### TASK B4: Transfer Coefficient (Ψ)

For the Spencer case \(f(x)=1\),

\[
\Psi_i = \frac{D_i + \lambda A_i}{D_i + \lambda A_i} = 1
\qquad\text{where}\quad
A_i = F\sin\alpha_i + \tan\phi'_i\cos\alpha_i,\;\;
D_i = F\cos\alpha_i - \tan\phi'_i\sin\alpha_i .
\]

**Evaluation at j=2 (slice 2):**  
\(F=1.541918,\; \lambda=0.233656\)  
Numerator at j=2: \(D_2 + \lambda A_2 = 1.6402\)  
Denominator at j=2: \(D_2 + \lambda A_2 = 1.6402\)  
Ψ₂ numerical value: **1.0**

### TASK B5: Interslice Function Indexing (Moment Equation)

In the moment‑equilibrium equation used to update \(\lambda\), the interslice function \(f(x)\) is evaluated at the **slice boundaries** (the left and right vertical interfaces of each slice). For slice \(k\) the two relevant evaluations are:

\[
f_{k} = f(x_{\text{left boundary}}),\qquad f_{k+1} = f(x_{\text{right boundary}}).
\]

They multiply the interslice normal forces as \(X_k = \lambda f_k E_k\) and \(X_{k+1} = \lambda f_{k+1} E_{k+1}\). In the Spencer assumption \(f(x)=1\), both equal 1.

### Observations

- **Sign of α and contribution to driving shear**  
  Slices 1–3 (α negative): base dips downward → contributes **positively** to the driving term (\(-W\sin\alpha > 0\)).  
  Slices 4–5 (α positive): base rises out of slope → contributes **negatively** (resisting).  
  In all slices the shear resistance is computed consistently through the recurrence.

- **Computation direction of Eⱼ**  
  The interslice normal forces \(E_j\) are computed **forward** (left to right, from slice 1 to slice n) using the recurrence \(E_{i+1}=E_i+\Theta_i\) with \(E_1=0\). The right‑end condition \(E_{n+1}=0\) provides the force‑equilibrium equation.

- **Maximum equilibrium residual at final iteration**  
  The convergence check used **max(|ΔF|, |Δλ|)** ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁶. The final force‑equilibrium residual \(E_6\) was **–48.2 kN**, and the moment residual **~0.1 kN·m**, both negligible relative to the scale of forces (~30 000 kN total weight).
